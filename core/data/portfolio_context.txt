--- Project 6: OCS — Operational Command System ---
Type:           Full-stack enterprise SaaS web platform (in development pipeline)
Stack:          TBD (REST API-capable web framework, RDBMS with ACID support, WebSockets,
                cloud storage pay-as-you-use, Meta Graph API integration)
Client:         Redsquare Creative Agency, Uganda (initial deployment; designed as agency-agnostic SaaS)
Status:         SRS completed and approved (Version 1.0); development commencing

Description:
  OCS is a bespoke creative workflow management platform built to replace the fragmented,
  error-prone operations of a creative agency — WhatsApp group chats, email threads, shared
  drives, and manual Excel tracking — with a single, structured, role-governed system. It manages
  every creative asset from brief creation through to post-deployment performance reporting.

  The system is architecturally designed as an agency-agnostic enterprise SaaS product — a white-
  label platform that can be licensed and re-skinned for different creative agencies without
  rebuilding the core. RedSquare's deployment is the first instance.

Core Features:
  1. Brief Intake & Ticket Management: No brief = no ticket, enforced without exception.
     Structured brief forms with mandatory fields; tickets auto-generated on submission.

  2. Ten-Stage Creative Pipeline: Strict sequential workflow enforcement — Brief Intake →
     Copywriting → Copy Review (internal) → Client Copy Review → Creative Director Copy Review
     → Design & Assets → Internal & Client Design Review → Final Sign-off → Deployment &
     Publishing → After Action Review (AAR). No stage can be skipped; no asset goes live
     without Stage 8 Creative Director sign-off.

  3. Campaign Management & Content Calendar: Multi-asset campaign planning with storytelling-
     based content sequencing, annual calendar import from Excel/Word, recurring campaign
     templates, and a client profitability view reconciling outputs with payment records.

  4. Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): 7 defined roles — System Administrator, Creative
     Director, Account Manager, Traffic Manager, Copywriter, Designer, Digital Marketer — each
     with strictly enforced, server-side-validated permission sets.

  5. The Love Note (Notification System): Scheduled, non-dismissible deadline alert system.
     Desktop push notifications 30 minutes before every deadline; persists until work is submitted
     or a time extension is approved by the Creative Director. Fully automated — no manual
     intervention required for routine alerts.

  6. Document Versioning & Autosave: Auto-save every ≤60 seconds; complete named version
     history (V1, V2, V3…) for all copy and design assets; no approved asset can ever be
     permanently deleted; multi-layer geographically separate backup architecture.

  7. Internal Communication Module: Real-time chat (WebSocket-based) replacing WhatsApp;
     broadcast announcements to all users or specific role groups; messages linkable to task records.

  8. Analytics & Reporting: Internal productivity reports (completion rates, turnaround times,
     bottleneck identification); automatic Meta Graph API pull for post-deployment performance
     data (reach, engagement, clicks, impressions); AAR case study generation; team portfolio
     profiles auto-built from completed work history.

  9. Audit Trail & Data Integrity: Tamper-proof, immutable audit log of every deletion,
     modification, approval, and role change. Deleted records are archived, never destroyed.
     Exportable as CSV or PDF for compliance review.

  10. Excel & Word Workflow Automation: Full import/export of content calendars, schedules,
      reports, briefs, and copy in .xlsx, .docx, and .pdf formats — replacing all existing
      manual spreadsheet workflows.

Non-functional Targets:
  - Page/dashboard load: ≤3 seconds (50 concurrent users)
  - Uptime: ≥99.5% during business hours (Mon–Fri, 7AM–10PM EAT)
  - Notification delivery: ≤10 seconds from scheduled trigger
  - Security: OWASP Top 10 compliance, bcrypt/Argon2 password hashing, TLS 1.2+, RBAC
    enforced server-side, account lockout after 5 failed login attempts

Relevance: Demonstrates enterprise SaaS architecture, complex multi-role workflow systems,
real-time communication, social media API integration, audit systems, and the ability to
translate detailed stakeholder requirements (full IEEE Std 830-1998 SRS) into a shippable product.
